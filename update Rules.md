Notes: Day 5 — Stage 0, silhouette & notan (2-value) design. Assigned ~3 hrs: 15 min warm-up lines/ellipses/boxes; 1 hr silhouette studies — 10 black-fill-only silhouettes of real props/vehicles/figures from reference, no interior detail, each must read instantly at thumbnail size (Didier's silhouette-first readability); 45 min notan — 3 two-value (pure black/white) breakdowns of film stills or master paintings, forcing every shape into one of two masses (Ruppel's big-shape design thinking); 45 min observational drawing from life — everyday objects drawn as accurate silhouettes first, then one interior value pass (McQue's real-world grounding; Kenneth Scott's fundamentals-as-anchor); 15 min self-review — flip each study to thumbnail: is the subject identifiable from silhouette alone, and does the notan still hold as two clean masses? Maps to Stage 0 exit criteria: shape design and value composition that read at a glance — the readability bar all later 3D work sits on. Failure mode flagged: symbol drawing — inventing generic silhouettes from memory instead of observing real proportions, and letting midtones creep into notan; if a study needs a third value or interior lines to read, restart it simpler.
